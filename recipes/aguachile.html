Aguachile

Aguachile, meaning "chili water" in Spanish, hails from the coastal regions of Mexico, particularly Sinaloa and Nayarit. It is a beloved dish, deeply rooted in the culinary heritage of these regions. Traditionally, Aguachile was prepared as a means to cool down and refresh after a day of fishing under the hot sun, making it a staple among fishermen and locals. Today, it has gained widespread popularity across Mexico and beyond for its mouthwatering flavors and invigorating qualities.

Aguachile Verde is a vibrant and invigorating Mexican dish that showcases the fresh flavors of seafood combined with the tanginess of lime and the heat of chili. This recipe offers a refreshing twist on traditional ceviche, as the seafood is marinated in a spicy lime-chili mixture, resulting in a burst of flavors that awaken the palate. Prepare to embark on a culinary journey filled with bold tastes and refreshing sensations.

Ingriedients

  • 1 pound fresh shrimp, peeled and deviened (or other seafood of your choice)
  • 4-5 limes, freshly squeezed (apporximately 1 cup of lime juice)
  • 2-3 serrano or jalapeno chillies, finely chopped (adjust to your preferred level of spiciness)
  • 1 cucumber, sliced or diced
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Clean and devein the shrimp, ensuring they are free from shells and veins. If using other seafood like fish or scallops, prepare the accordingly.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the freshly squeezed lime juice and the finely chopped serrano or jalapeno chilies. Adjust the amount of chillies to suit your taste for spiciness.
  3. Place the shrimp or seafood of your choice into the lime-chilli mixture, ensuring it is fully coated. Allow the seafood to marinate for approximately 10-15 minutes. The acidity of the lime juice will "cook" the seafood, resulting in a tender and flavorful texture.
  4. Meanwhile, prepare the remaing ingredients. Slice or dice the cucumber, thinly slice the red onion, and chop the cilantro leaves.
  5. After marinating, remove the shrimp or seafood from the lime-chilli mixture, reserving the liquid for later use.
  6. Arrange the marinated seafood on a serving plate, creating a single layer.
  7. Scatter the sliced cucmber, red onion, and chopped cilantro over the seafood, distributing them evenly.
  8. Season the dish with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Drizzle some of the reserved lime-chilli mixture over the seafood, ensuring it is evenly coated. Reserve the rest to serve as a dipping sauce.
  10. Serve immediately, allowing the vibrant flavors to shine
